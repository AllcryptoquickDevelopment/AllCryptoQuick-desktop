Wallet setup
Create or restore wallet In order to connect to Particl network, you need to have a wallet. If you're using Particl for the first time, create a new wallet or restore existing wallet (using your Recovery Passphrase) if you have one already. You can create as many wallets as you need, for example to separate your funds.

Name your wallet Naming your wallets is useful, when you want to use multiple wallets – this helps you to separate e.g. your personal and market funds.
Short and descriptive names work best – for example: Personal, Work, Savings etc. Note that your wallet will be created on successful completion of this step, but still requires further configuration to be usable.

Encrypting your wallet

Recovery Phrase for '{{ currentWalletName }}' wallet Recovery Phrase (sometimes also called "wallet seed" or "mnemonic") is a 24-word backup key to your coins. In the case of emergency, it can be used to restore your wallet (e.g. on different machine). You can further increase security of your Recovery Phrase in the next step. Confirm Recovery Phrase Confirm that you backed up your Recovery Phrase by filling in the missing words: If you're not absolutely sure, that you have all Recovery Phrase words correctly saved, you can always go one step back (this will generate completely new Recovery Phrase) and start over. Restore existing '{{ currentWalletName }}' wallet For restoring your existing wallet, you will need its Recovery Phrase (and also Recovery Password if you have set it up): Friendly reminder: Don't forget that the Recovery Phrase (and Password) are very important for keeping your funds safe. Anyone with access to them has access to your coins. If you think your Recovery Phrase (and Password) might have been exposed, it would be better to create a new wallet instead and send your coins there. Choose a Recovery Password (optional) You can further increase your wallet protection by setting a Recovery Password.
It will be needed together with Recovery Phrase to access your coins. Note that you can safely skip this step, but it is recommended to choose a Recovery Password as it keeps your coins safer.

You should

  • Write down these words – physically, e.g. on paper (or ideally on specialized cryptocurrency metal seed backup products)!
  • Keep them in a safe place – make sure they won't be destroyed by elements like water!
  • Backup them on multiple locations if possible!

You should not

  • Save them digitally – on your computer, mobile or cloud storages!
  • Give them to anyone – they could steal your funds!
  • Lose them – you could lose access to your coins in the future!
